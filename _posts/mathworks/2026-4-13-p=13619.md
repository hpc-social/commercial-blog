<h2 id="1">Query</h2>
<p>Professor Magdy Hanna of the Department of Engineering Mathematics and Physics at Fayoum University in Fayoum, Egypt, asked me:</p>

<pre>  If we have the QR decomposition of a matrix, with or without
  pivoting, is there an easy way to get the condition number of
  the matrix without starting from scratch and using the SVD?</pre>
<h2 id="2">Short answer</h2>
<p>The short answer to Prof. Hanna's question is:</p>

<pre>  Yes.  The ratio of the largest and smallest diagonal elements
  of qr(A) is often a good estimate of cond(A).</pre>
<p>Compare this estimate to the exact condition of a magic square. Produce a <tt>score</tt> measuring how well the estimate does.</p>

<pre class="codeinput">     A = magic(11);
     R = qr(A);
     d = abs(diag(R));
     est = max(d)/min(d);
     kappa = cond(A);
     disp(<span class="string">"       est     kappa     score"</span>)
     disp([est kappa est/kappa])
</pre>
<pre class="codeoutput"> 

       est     kappa     score
    2.6964   11.1021    0.2429

</pre>
<h2 id="3">QR Decomposition</h2>
<p>A more complete answer involves column pivoting.</p>

<p>The QR decomposition <b>without</b> column pivoting is an orthogonal Q and an upper triangular R so that</p>

<pre>  A = Q*R</pre>
<p>The QR composition <b>with</b> column pivoting is an orthogonal Q, an upper triangular R and a permutation P that reorders the columns of A so that</p>

<pre>  A*P = Q*R</pre>
<p>In either case, Q and P are orthogonal, so</p>

<pre>  cond(R) = cond(A)</pre>
<p>Any estimate of the condition of R provides an estimate of the condition of A.</p>

<h2 id="4">tricond</h2>
<p>An estimate the condition of a triangular matrix is the ratio of the largest and smallest elements on the diagonal.</p>

<pre class="codeinput">   type <span class="string">tricond</span>
</pre>
<pre class="codeoutput">

     function cond = tricond(T)
        % Estimate condition of lower or upper triangular matrix.
        if istril(T) || istriu(T)
            D = diag(abs(T));
            cond = max(D)/min(D);
        else
            cond =  NaN;
        end
     end
</pre>
